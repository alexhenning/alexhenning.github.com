Setting up an RSS feed

Once you've set up a blog, you might want to set up an RSS feed. And if you don't, well then, too bad! Setting up an RSS feed is quite simple with jekyll. First create a file called atom.xml. 

> touch atom.xml

Next, just copy the following into atom.xml, changing username, email and the site to fit your site 

 1:  ---
 2:  layout: nil
 3:  ---
 4:  <?xml version="1.0" encoding="utf-8"?>
 5:  <feed xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2005/Atom">
 6:  
 7:   <title>Alex Henning</title>
 8:   <link href="http://alexhenning.github.com/atom.xml" rel="self"/>
 9:   <link href="http://alexhenning.github.com/"/>
10:   <updated>{{ site.time | date_to_xmlschema }}</updated>
11:   <id>http://alexhenning.github.com/</id>
12:   <author>
13:     <name>Alex Henning</name>
14:     <email>your@email.com</email>
15:   </author>
16:  
17:   {% for post in site.posts limit:25 %}
18:   <entry>
19:     <title>{{ post.title }}</title>
20:     <link href="http://alexhenning.github.com{{ post.url }}"/>
21:     <updated>{{ post.date | date_to_xmlschema }}</updated>
22:     <id>http://alexhenning.github.com{{ post.id }}</id>
23:     <content type="html">{{ post.content | xml_escape }}</content>
24:   </entry>
25:   {% endfor %}
26:  
27:  </feed>

It's a pretty plain file, mostly just saying that it's an atom feed. However, from line 17 to line 25, jekyll runs a loop that creates an atom <entry> for the last 25 posts posted. You can remove limit:25 to show all posts ever, but that would become obnoxious after you got a large number of posts. Just pick a number that seems reasonable and go with it.
